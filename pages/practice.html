TACHEOMETRIC SURVEYING

Tacheometry is a branch of angular surveying in which both the horizontal and vertical distances of points are
obtained by optical means as opposed to the ordinary slower process of measurement by tape or chain.
In this method, measurements by tape or chain is completely dispensed with horizontal distances obtained by
tacheometry observations do not require slope correction
,tension correction etc. This method is very rapid and convenient. It is the best
adopted in obstacles such as steep and broken ground, deep revines, stretches of water or swamp and so on, which
make chaining difficult or impossible.

PURPOSE OF TACHEOMETRIC SURVEYING

The primary object of tacheometry is the preparation of contoured maps or plans.
It is considered to be rapid and accurate in rough country and country and has thus been widely used by engineers in the location
survey for railway,canals, reservoirs,etc.
Whenever survey of higher accuracy are carried out,tacheometry provides a good check on distances measured with a tape or chain.

USES OF TACHEOMETRY AND INSTRUMENTS USED FOR TACHEOMETRY SURVEYING

The following are the uses of Tacheometry
  • Detail Filling
  • Establishing Secondary control
  • Checking of already measured distances

The following are the instruments used for tacheometric surveying
  1. Tacheometer
  2. Stadia rods

LOCAL ATTRACTION

North end of a freely suspended magnetic needle always points to the magnetic north, if it is not
influenced by any other external forces except the earth magnetic field. It is common experience that
the magnetic needle gets deflected from its normal position if placed near magnetic rocks, iron ores,
cables carrying current or electric poles, such a disturbing force is known as LOCAL ATTRACTION. For more details on Local Attraction
The presence of local attraction in any station may be detected by observing the FB and BB of the line.
If the difference between FB and BB is 180degrees, both stations are free from local attraction.
If not the discrepancy may be due to the following;

An error in the observations of either FB or BB or both

Presence of local attraction at either stations

Presence of local attraction at both stations
